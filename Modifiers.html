·         Modifiers – phrases that modify a noun

·         Dangling modifiers – don’t modify anything. The subject that is supposed to be modified is missing and replaced with a different subject

o   Put the noun being modified in the right place in the sentence

o   Put the noun being modified onto the noun itself

o   You can’t fix a dangling modifier by rearranging the words

·         Misplaced modifiers – modifier that is awkwardly placed in a sentence, so it modifies something else in the sentence

o   Most commonly misplaced modifier is “only”

o   Modifier should go directly before what it is modifying

·         Almost and nearly

o   Use “almost” and “nearly” before a noun when you started but didn’t finish an action

§  I ate almost an entire pizza

o   Use “almost” and “nearly” before a verb when you got close to, but didn’t start, an action

§  I almost ate an entire pizza

·         Split infinitive – misplaced modifier when adverb is placed between “to” and the verb
