·         The pause trick – put a comma where you have to breathe

o   Pause trick doesn’t work

Comma Usages (will be on final)

·         Use a comma with FANBOYS to link two independent clauses

·         Use a comma after an introduction leading to an independent clause

o   The intro itself cannot be an independent clause

o   One-word introductions

§  However, therefore, undoubtedly, similarly, etc.

·         Between all items on a list of three or more

·         When two coordinate adjectives modify a noun

o   “The large, hungry dog ran after the scrawny, quick cat.”

·         Around interruptions and “by the way” information, but not around necessary and identifying information

o   Required information removes ambiguity

§  “My brother John is an attorney.” - John is necessary identifying information.

§  “My oldest brother, John, is an attorney.” - “Oldest brother” is identifying, so commas go around John

§  “My favorite brother, John, is an attorney.” - “favorite brother” is identifying

o   Which vs. That – information introduced with a “which” is surrounded by commas. Information introduced with a “that” is not surrounded by commas.

§  “My neighbor’s car alarm, which is silent now, woke me up last night.” - here, “which” is not identifying which car, so we use commas

§  “The goldfish that my cousin won at a carnival lived for ten years.” - here, “that” is identifying which goldfish, so we don’t need commas

·         Misc.

o   To offset a contrast with “but”

§  “I was exhausted and cold, but happy.”

o   Around years if an exact date is given

§  “We got married on July 25, 1998, in front of our family and friends.”

o   Around country and state names if the city is named.

§  “I was Mallorca, Spain, but later moved to Los Angeles, California, to pursue a career in acting.”
