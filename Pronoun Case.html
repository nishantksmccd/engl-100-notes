·         Personal pronouns – refer to specific persons or things

o   I, me, you, he, him, she, her, it, we, us, they, them

·         Pronoun case error – when a personal pronoun is doing the wrong type of job

o   Every sentence needs a verb

o   Not every personal pronoun is allowed to do a verb

·         Subject pronouns do verbs, object pronouns don’t do verbs (will be on final)

·         To figure out which verb to use when there are multiple people, get rid of the other people

o   The teacher questioned Helen and Me.

o   The teacher questioned Me.

·         Reflexive pronoun – pronoun ending in -self or –selves

o   Used when the doer (subject) and recipient (object) of the action are the same person

§  I injured myself

§  She climbed it by herself

o   Do not use myself in place of I or me

·         Implied verbs – when a verb is implied

o   I wish I were as smart as she – implied verb

§  I wish I were as smart as she is.

o   I can run as fast as he (can)

o   Use subject pronouns if the verb is absent or implied

·         When “to be” is used as a linking verb

o   Use subject pronouns if the pronoun and antecedent are on opposite sides of “is,” “are,” “were”

§  Refer to the same thing

o   Replace equals sign with where “to be” or equivalent would be

§  “This = she” or “It = I”

§  “I can’t believe you are he”

·         Gerund – a noun that ends in –ing

o   Running is boring – implied that “running” is the subject

o   Because of the pollen in the air, my sneezing became uncontrollable.
