Run-on Sentences and Comma Splices

ENGL 100

Niko Kompella

 

  • Run-ons don’t have anything to do with length
  • Seven Coordinate conjunctions:
    • For
    • And
    • Nor
    • But
    • Or
    • Yet
    • So
    • Use FANBOYS acronym
  • Coordinate conjunction – link items of equal grammatical value (will be on final)
    • Links words, phrases, and clauses
  • Run-on sentence – two independent clauses improperly linked
    • I have a dog his name is Rocko – run-on
    • I have a dog and his name is Rocko – run-on
    • I have a dog, his name is Rocko – Comma splice
  • Comma Splice – a type of run-on wherein two independent clauses are linked with only a comma
  • How to link two independent clauses
    • Use a comma + FANBOYS
      • I have a dog, and his name is Rocko.
    • Use a semicolon
      • I have a dog; his name is Rocko.
      • Using a semicolon implies a connection between the two clauses
      • Use semicolons only between two independent clauses and sparingly
    • Make two sentences
      • I have a dog. His name is Rocko.
    • Rewrite the sentence so you have only one independent clause
      • I have a dog named Rocko.
      • You can turn one of the clauses into a subordinate clause with “because” or another subordinating conjunction
