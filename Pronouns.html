·         Pronoun – a word that takes place of a noun

·         Antecedent – noun a pronoun replaces

·         When a pronoun replaces a noun:

o   Pronoun must agree with the antecedent regarding number (single vs. plural)

o   Must be clear what the pronoun is replacing

·         Pronoun agreement error – when a pronoun and its antecedent don’t agree

·         Indefinite pronoun – non-specific persons or things

o   Anybody, anything each, everybody, nobody, somebody, something, etc.

o   Treat indefinite pronouns as singular

·         Collective nouns – name a group

o   Corporation, jury, team, crowd, family, audience, committee, class, etc.

o   Treat collective nouns as singular

·         Pronoun reference error – it is unclear what the corresponding antecedent to a pronoun is

o   Can be solved by restating the antecedent instead of a pronoun

o   Can be solved by reorganizing sentence structure

o   Can be solved by directly quoting, if there is ambiguity about who said what
